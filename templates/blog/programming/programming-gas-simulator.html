Gas simulator (Link to GitHub)

The idea

This project was an attempt to construct a gas simulator. The particles that conforms an ideal do not interact among each other. Otherwise, a real gas has interactions among its particles. A possible model could be the a Coulomb force that will be seen as a repulsion force. To address such propose, the equations of motion of each particle were obtained from the Coulomb force: \begin{equation}\label{force} F_i(\vec{r}_i(t))= \frac{q^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\sum_{i < j}\frac{\hat{r}_i(t)}{|\vec{r}_i(t)-\vec{r}_j(t)|^2} \end{equation} The equation \ref{force} was used to find the velocity of each particle as: \begin{equation} \vec{v}_i(t)=\frac{1}{m_i}\frac{q^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\sum_{i < j}\int_0^{t} dt \frac{\hat{r}_i(t)}{|\vec{r}_i(t)-\vec{r}_j(t)|^2}+\vec{v}_i(0) \end{equation} where $m_i$ is the mass of each particle. $\vec{v}_i(0)$ is the initial condition of velocities.

How it works?

Yo must insert in the field the number of particles that you wanna render. Then, click "enter"

In the dropdown menu, select the interaction and click in simulate. The fastest particle, more red it is and the darkest are slower than the red one.
